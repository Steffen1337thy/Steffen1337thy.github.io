My Data Science Journey!
Where it began - where it will go...

Phase 1 - Starting my career! I started as a bank advisor. But at any point I discovered that I was more interested in finding
general approaches to sell specific products to target groups than in individual selling processes with customers.
I had a good feeling about small-talk topics as indicators of needs for different products.
I also built some simple calculation and visualization tools that improved my closing efficiency.
So I quit my job to study and find a job that requires more analytical and strategical thinking.

Phase 2 - Studying I made my bachelor in Economics. During my studies I discovered my love for math, statistics and computer science.
While I worked as Tutor for math and statistics, I underestimated my talent in programming.
The computer science course lasted two semesters and focused on sql and vba.
It was very easy but I thought I am just good "for an economist" in it. I don't know yet, why the field
of data science never came up to me. After my bachelor I started my Master in Sales Management.
The Master program was limited for 15 students per semester and I wanted to get more specialized.
Luckily for me, a lot of the courses had a focus on statistics and so I got into work with several analytical tools like MPlus and SPSS.

Phase 3 - First Job after study! As mentioned before I wanted to work on more analytical and strategic topics.
So after finishing my Master I joined the Sales Coordination & Business Development Department of tkre.
I worked on several topics in the fields of MI, BI and Business Development and Pricing.
Due to my affinity to Statistics and Programming I got full control of the Sales BI.
As I searched for possibilities to scale up methods and skills of statistical analysis for greater
data I discovered the field of data science. So my taught myself

Phase 4 - Getting deeper into data! Since I found out about the wide field of data science I jumped
fully into it. Due to my studies I had a strong foundation in statistics. So I started with teaching myself python
and getting a stronger knowledge in computer science.
I started with the cs50 course on edx.org. This was really fun and challenging. After building up
more knowledge with the help of udemy (I can really recommend xy if you are german speaker) and youtube
I started to integrate programming in my daily working business.
Building pipelines, cleaning data, finding duplicates across different tables I was able to
integrate programming in my daily business.
To further improve my skills I worked myself through different literature (especially O'Reilly) ,
my daily reading feed on medium and started my micromaster in Data Science via MITx. I already finished half of the courses.

Phase 5 - What's next? The next thing is projects, projects and more projects.
The best way to improve from this point is in working on projects. And it is also the goal. To make cool stuff with data :-)
